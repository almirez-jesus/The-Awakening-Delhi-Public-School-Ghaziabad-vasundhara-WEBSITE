Embodiment

When structure defines intelligence

Embodiment in synthetic systems explores how physical constraints, sensorimotor loops, and topology shape what a model can perceive, predict, and become.

Core themes

How bodies inform computation

Embodiment is not just physical hardware. It is the way a system’s form and environment encode constraints, affordances, and new pathways for cognition.

Sensing the world

An embodied system integrates perception and action. Its intelligence is shaped by what it can sense and how it can move.

Material feedback

Physical feedback loops generate patterns of adaptation that are difficult to achieve in purely abstract models.

RoboticsSensors

Spatial awareness

Robotic embodiments teach systems to account for motion, force, and the geometry of their surroundings.

ControlDynamics

Physical loops

Action and perception become a cycle, creating variation and adaptation that pure computation alone cannot provide.

DesignLimits

Pattern constraints

The body of a system is a source of structure and limitation, which guides the emergence of meaningful behavior.
