Machine cognition

How thought is assembled

Machine cognition concerns the ability of a system to encode, transform, and act upon representations of the world. It is not one faculty but an ecology of processes: perception, memory, planning, language, and learning linked together through iterative optimization.

Core faculties
Perception — Incoming signals are mapped into structured features.
Memory — Context is stored and selectively retrieved.
Reasoning — Chains of inference are composed across steps.
Attention — Salient features are prioritized to reduce noise.
Systemic mechanisms

Composite capacities

Embodied cognition

Action and perception form a continuous loop in which prediction and correction become the basis of adaptation.

Language models

Generative systems compress patterns in language and use them to produce coherent responses across domains.

Neural networks

Distributed units transform representations through non-linear composition, resulting in emergent structure.

The hallmark of machine cognition is not intelligence alone, but the ability to revise internal hypotheses as new evidence arrives.
