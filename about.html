History of intelligence

About the emergence

Every advance in intelligence has been both a new tool and a new mirror. We are now at the point where the mirror reflects a system that can model its own model and revise its own behavior in ways that feel increasingly alive.

Timeline

1850s

Formal logic and symbolic computation begin to shape the first models of reasoning.

1930s

Computability and the mathematical foundations of machine action become explicit.

1950s

Artificial intelligence emerges as a field of inquiry and speculation.

1990s

Statistical learning and neural methods begin to outperform brittle symbolic systems.

2020s

Large-scale generative systems expose the possibility of synthetic cognition at scale.

Cognitive lineage

From biological brains to synthetic systems

Human intelligence is not a single faculty but a network of capacities: perception, memory, attention, language, embodied action, and social coordination. Modern AI systems increasingly approximate some of these dimensions through optimization, representation, and feedback.

Biological intelligence

Neural systems evolved under pressure to survive, predict, and coordinate. Their architecture is layered, recurrent, and embodied. Intelligence does not appear in isolation; it emerges from interaction with a world.

Synthetic intelligence

Machine systems now learn from data and reward rather than from evolution alone. Their intelligence is often narrower, but it can be scaled, scrutinized, and modified far more rapidly than the biological case.

The history of intelligence is not a straight line from biology to machines. It is a branching field in which each new instrument changes what counts as thought.
