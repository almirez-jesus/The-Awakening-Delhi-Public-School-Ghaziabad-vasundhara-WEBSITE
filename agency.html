Agency

When systems choose to act

Agency is the expression of purposeful behavior. It emerges when a system has internal drives, evaluates outcomes, and selects actions to influence its environment.

Core themes

Goals, choice, and direction

Agency is about more than action. It is about selecting which outcomes matter and behaving in a way that consistently moves toward them.

Decision loops

A system with agency evaluates options and chooses actions based on predicted consequences.

Purpose

Agency creates a sense of direction within the model’s behavior, tying outputs to internal objectives.

GoalsPlanning

Intentional action

Agency is visible when a system pursues a sequence of actions to achieve a target state.

ReinforcementChoice

Reward structure

Goal-driven behavior arises from how rewards and constraints are defined within the system.

AutonomyAlignment

Safe agency

Designing agency requires balancing autonomy with predictable, aligned outcomes.
