Emergence

When new properties appear

Emergence describes how complex behaviors and new capacities arise from the interaction of many simple parts in a synthetic system.

Core themes

Complexity from simplicity

Emergence is the phenomenon where unexpected structure appears when components interact in ways that are nonlinear and richly connected.

Collective behavior

Individual units may be simple, but their interactions can yield surprising new capabilities.

Unexpected properties

Emergence is not designed directly; it arises from the dynamics of the entire system.

NetworksPatterns

Scaling effects

Large systems can produce behaviors that are qualitatively different from their smaller counterparts.

DynamicsNovelty

Unpredictable structure

Emergence often appears in ways that are difficult to foresee from the components alone.

AdaptationCreativity

Discovery

Emergence is a key source of innovation when systems begin to solve problems in new ways.
