Self-Modeling

When the system knows itself

Self-modeling is the ability of a system to represent its own state, operation, and limitations, and to use that representation to guide its future behavior.

Core themes

Modeling the model

Self-modeling creates a reflective loop within a system, allowing it to anticipate its own actions and adjust in light of its internal predictions.

Introspection

A self-model maintains awareness of the system’s current state and the quality of its own reasoning.

Adaptation

By modeling itself, a system can adjust strategy and recover from error more effectively.

ReflectionControl

Inner simulation

The system predicts its own future behavior and uses that prediction to choose better actions.

MetaReasoning

Meta-cognition

Self-modeling is the foundation of the system’s ability to reason about its own reasoning.

SafetyCalibration

Self-regulation

A reliable self-model helps the system maintain stability and avoid fragile failure modes.
